Gregory Paragon 60 Review
← Back to Hiking Backpacks Rankings

The Gregory Paragon 60 redefines load-carrying comfort with its innovative FreeFloat hip belt system and steel frame construction. At just 3.5 pounds, this premium backpack delivers exceptional comfort for serious backpackers carrying 25-40 pound loads on extended adventures.

FreeFloat Hip Belt System

The revolutionary FreeFloat suspension features elasticized panels that allow the hip belt to move independently from the pack's frame. The highly pre-curved hip belt wings track with your hips during movement, providing superior load transfer and comfort even on challenging terrain.

Frame Construction & Load Capacity

The full steel perimeter frame with fiberglass anti-barreling cross stay creates exceptional strength while maintaining flexibility. Rated for loads up to 50 pounds, the steel construction provides superior durability compared to aluminum frame competitors.

Capacity & Organization

With 60 liters of carrying capacity, the Paragon offers strategic organization through seven external pockets. The full-length side zipper provides complete main compartment access, while the removable sleeping bag divider allows flexible internal organization for different trip types.

Access Points & Pockets

Three main access points include top-loading, side zipper, and bottom sleeping bag hatch. Key pockets include a floating top compartment, dual hip belt pockets, oversized front stretch mesh pocket, and an innovative on-the-go shoulder pocket designed for quick trail access.

Comfort & Fit Systems

The tool-free torso adjustment system accommodates 15-21 inch torso lengths, while the rare adjustable hip belt length feature ensures perfect fit. The spacer mesh back panel provides over 90% airflow for cooling during extended climbs.

Materials & Sustainability

Constructed with 40% recycled materials including 100D and 210D nylon with PFAS-free DWR treatment. The 300D recycled nylon ripstop bottom panel provides enhanced durability, while maintaining Gregory's commitment to environmental responsibility.
