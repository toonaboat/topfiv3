Soundcore Space One Review
← Back to Wireless Headphones Rankings

In a market flooded with premium ANC headphones, the Soundcore Space One stands out for delivering top-tier sound, comfort, and battery life at a highly competitive price. Here's how they stack up.

Sound Quality

Powered by 40mm drivers and LDAC support, these deliver deep bass, crisp highs, and wide soundstage clarity. Even non-audiophiles and those streaming from platforms like Spotify get impressive detail without premium hardware.

Noise Cancellation

While not quite on par with Bose or Sony, the ANC performs admirably—especially against drones like air conditioners or traffic. It consistently dampens ambient noise, though abrupt or conversation sounds may still break through.

Build & Comfort

Lightweight with rotating earcups and plush earpads, the Space One is comfortable for long sessions. The only issue is sweat accumulation in hot environments and a plastic finish that may feel less rugged.

Battery Life

Impressive endurance: around 40 hours with ANC on or up to 55 hours without. Quick charging and battery alerts add to the convenience.

Features & App

The Soundcore app offers EQ, ANC adjustment, transparency mode, wear detection, and device switching. Some users note occasional pausing quirks, especially post-sweat.

Call Quality

Voice calls are clear and functional for daily use, though not studio-grade. Background noise is mostly filtered out, making them acceptable for virtual meetings or chats.

Value for Money

Around $99 (often less on sale), the Space One punches well above its price. While not perfect, they offer premium-level ANC and sound without a premium price point.
