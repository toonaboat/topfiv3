Sony WH-1000XM6 Review: Still the ANC King in 2025?
← Back to Wireless Headphones Rankings

If you're hunting for wireless headphones that blend top-tier noise cancellation, customizable sound, and practical comfort, the Sony WH-1000XM6 are hard to ignore. This latest model in Sony's WH-1000X series delivers meaningful refinements that make it a standout pick for anyone serious about their audio.

Build Quality & Design

The XM6 headphones showcase a practical evolution of Sony's minimalist design. They're lightweight, foldable, and equipped with a more supportive headband and softer, pressure-free earcups. The magnetic closure case is a welcomed upgrade over previous zippered versions. While the plastic build doesn't scream luxury, it's perfectly functional and travel-friendly. For fewer smudges, we recommend the Platinum Silver finish over the darker options.

Sound Quality

Out of the box, the XM6 deliver a balanced and enjoyable sound signature. But the real advantage is Sony's Headphones Connect app, which gives you a 10-band EQ to shape your sound. Once dialed in, these headphones rival (and often beat) flagship models like the AirPods Max or Bose QuietComfort Ultra. LDAC support also ensures hi-res playback — provided your device supports it.

Noise Cancellation & Features

The ANC performance is exceptional. A 12-microphone system and Sony's QN3 processor handle everything from background chatter to engine hum with ease. Gesture controls, "speak-to-chat," adaptive sound modes, and multi-device pairing make these not only powerful, but smart.

Comfort & Usability

The XM6 offer all-day comfort with moderate clamping force and pillowy earcups. The fit might feel snug for larger ears, but fatigue is minimal even during extended listening. Wear detection and quick-access controls also improve usability.

Battery Life & Charging

Expect up to 30 hours of playback with ANC on. A quick 3-minute charge nets you 3 hours of use. You can charge and use them simultaneously — but consider a longer cable, as the one provided is fairly short.
